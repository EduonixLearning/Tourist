• Elephanta Caves lie just off the coast of the Arabian Sea, situated at a 10 km radius from the bustling metropolis.
  • Elephanta Caves are the perfect expressions of archaic Indian art associated to the cult of Lord Shiva.
  • The island on which the caves are built was originally known as Gharapuri, and the Portuguese retitled it as Elephanta Island when they discovered a large stone structure of an Elephant on the island.
  • The temple was primarily built for the worship of Lord Shiva and 'Shivaism'.
  • The Elephanta Island is a quiet and picturesque island with green foliage and harmless primates scampering about.
  • A paradise for women, this little market offers sculptures made of marble, jewelery, clothes, paintings of Lord Shiva and many amazing items of wood work.
  • If you are interested in local Indian food, an MTDC run resort offers a fine local spread at a reasonable cost.
  • One can also enjoy the scenic beauty of this island.
  • Rs 10/- per person for Indians
  • Rs 250/- per person for Foreigners
  • All days of the week except Monday
  • 9:00 am to 5.00 pm

By Road:
  • From Churchgate or CST, one can walk to Gateway of India along the heritage lane. Its a 20 to 30 minute walk. Or a 10 minute taxi ride.
  • Once in Gateway of India, you are just a 60 minute away from Elephanta by the ferry.

By Rail:
  • One should desembark at CST station or Churchgate station from there just reach to Gateway of India.
By Ferry/Boat:
  • To go to Elephanta Caves, one will have to go to the Gateway of India in Mumbai and take a boat/ferry ride from there.
  • Tickets for a deluxe boat are Rs. 140 for adults and Rs. 90 for children.
  • Buy the ticket and ask for the direction to board the boat.
  • If a boat is not already boarding, you may need to wait till the next boat arrives.
  • Typically every 30 minutes a boat leaves for Elephanta.
  • Make sure that you are boarding the Elephanta boat rather than the local sightseeing boat.
  • The first boat leaves at 9:00 AM and the last boat from the island leaves at 5:00 PM.
  • The journey takes one hour by sea.
